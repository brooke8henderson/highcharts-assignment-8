Trends in U.S. Foreign Aid

By Brooke Henderson

In 2007, President George Bush entered into a 10-year Memoradum of Understanding with Israel to allocate over $30 billion of the U.S. budget to funding Israeli military initiatives. As a result, Israel is the largest recipient of U.S. aid, with Egypt in second place and Afghanistan following behind as third. Because of the importance of foreign aid in the U.S. budget, Congress has required the United States Agency for International Development to submit a report annually since 1945, according to the USAID website.

Source: The USAID Foreign Assistance Database "U.S. Overseas Loans and Grants" report to Congress, Aid Trends by Sector.
